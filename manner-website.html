The basic etiquette guide to your next trip to Japan



ABOUT


"Good etiquette is the act of aligning yourself with the culture and tradition of a place."


Cultural etiquette is what you call the codes of behavior that rule different cultures – in other words, what's acceptable and what isn't in a society. Its kin, culture shock, is what travelers experience when faced with irreconcilable cultural differences. To avoid such issue, Manabu is here to help you be prepared with decent Japanese etiquette knowledge before your trip to Japan.
